Boudica

Historical Background

Iceni Tribe

  • The Iceni were a British Celtic Tribe
  • Led by King Prasutagus till his death in ~60-61 B.C.

Roman Conquest

  • The Romans began conquering Celtic tribes in A.D. 43
  • King Prasutagus surrendered / allied to Rome

Gaius Suetonius Paulinus

  • Roman Provincial Governor of Britain
  • The primary enemy of the local Celtic tribes

Publius Cornelius Tacitus

  • Primary historian
  • His father in law served under Paulinus

Personal Background

  • Boudica was born in ~30 A.D. to a wealthy family
  • Like other Celtic women, she was trained as a warrior
  • She married King Prasutagus Perhaps she was attracted to that crazy name of his...

Timeline

Prasutagus' Death

  • Prasutagus had no male heir to inherit the kingdom
  • So he passed on his kingdom to Boudica, their daughters, and Emperor Nero

Roman Annexation of Iceni

  • After Prasutagus' death, Romans took over Iceni
  • They flogged Boudica and raped her daughters

Vengeance!

"Nothing is safe from Roman pride and arrogance. They will deface the sacred and will deflower our virgins. Win the battle or perish, that is what I, a woman, will do." -- Boudica

Paulinus Assaults the Island of Mona

  • Provincial Governor Paulinus leaves to go conquer the island of Mona around ~60-61 A.D.
  • Boudica takes advantage of his absence and starts attacking

Boudica Begins Attack

  • She manages to raise an army of ~230,000 soldiers
  • Her army slaughters ~70,000-80,000 Romans
  • They also destroy the cities of Camulodunum, Londinium, and Verulamium

Paulinus Returns

  • Sometime during the destruction of these cities, Paulinus returns
  • He regroups his army, and has about ~10,000 soldiers

The Battle

  • Paulinus chooses the battlefield carefully to take advantage of the Roman's superior skill at warfare
  • His troops are outnumbered about 23-1
  • Boudica brings all her forces in
  • Her troops even bring along family with the supply wagons

Boudica's Defeat

  • Roman skill trumps Boudica's sheer might
  • Roman troops better at close quarter fighting
  • Boudica's soldiers were unable to retreat because of their supply wagons

Battle Aftermath

  • The Romans kill ~80,000 Britons
  • Boudica commits suicide by drinking poison

Boudica's Legacy

Recognition

  • Although Boudica wasn't given a lot of recognition until the 1500's, she has had many poems, statues, boats, and other namesakes created since then
  • Shown is Boadicea and Her Daughters

Modern Day Parallels

  • Boudica was a queen who stood up to abuses of power by an oppressive government
  • There are a lot of parallels between what she faced and what we currently experience (especially now in 2020)
  • While hopefully none of us have to go through what she went through, Boudica still shines as an example of dedication and commitment to freedom and to her people
