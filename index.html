Boudica

Historical Background

Iceni Tribe

  • British Celtic Tribe
  • Led by King Prasutagus

Roman Conquest

  • Romans began conquering Celtic tribes in A.D. 43
  • Prasutagus surrendered / allied to Rome

Gaius Suetonius Paulinus

  • Roman Provincial Governor of Britain

Publius Cornelius Tacitus

  • Primary historian
  • Father in Law served under Paulinus

Personal Background

  • Born ~30 A.D. to a wealthy family
  • Like other Celtic women, was trained as a warrior
  • Married King Prasutagus Perhaps she was attracted to that crazy name of his...

Timeline

Prasutagus' Death

  • No male heir
  • Passed on kingdom to Boudica, their daughters, and Emperor Nero

Roman Annexation of Iceni

  • Romans took over Iceni
  • Flogged Boudica and raped her daughters

Vengeance!

"Nothing is safe from Roman pride and arrogance. They will deface the sacred and will deflower our virgins. Win the battle or perish, that is what I, a woman, will do."

Paulinus Assaults the Island of Mona

  • Leaves to go conquer the island in ~60-61 A.D.
  • Boudica takes advantage of his absence

Boudica Begins Attack

  • Manages to raise an army of ~230,000 soldiers
  • Slaughters ~70,000-80,000 Romans
  • Destroys the cities of Camulodunum, Londinium, and Verulamium

Paulinus Returns

  • Sometime during the destruction of these cities, Paulinus returns
  • Regroups army, ~10,000 soldiers

The Battle

  • Paulinus chooses the battlefield carefully to take advantage of the Roman's superior skill at warfare
  • His troops are outnumbered about 23-1
  • Boudica brings all her forces in
  • Her troops even bring along family with the supply wagons

Boudica's Defeat

  • Roman skill trumps Boudica's sheer might
  • Roman troops better at close quarter fighting
  • Boudica's soldiers unable to retreat because of their supply wagons

Battle Aftermath

  • Romans kill ~80,000 Britons
  • Boudica commits suicide by drinking poison
